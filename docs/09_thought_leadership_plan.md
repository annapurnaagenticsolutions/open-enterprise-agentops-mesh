# 09. Thought Leadership Plan

## Purpose

This plan turns the open-source project into industry presence. The objective is to be seen as a practical enterprise AI / AgentOps architect, not only as a developer or content creator.

## Positioning line

> I help enterprises move from GenAI pilots to governed, measurable, production-grade AI agents.

## Audience

Primary:

- CIOs / CTOs
- Data and analytics leaders
- Enterprise architects
- AI product leaders
- Platform engineering leaders
- Governance and risk teams

Secondary:

- AI engineers
- Consultants
- Startup founders
- Product managers
- Learning communities

## Content pillars

| Pillar | Message |

## Launch announcement draft

Title:

**Introducing Open Enterprise AgentOps Mesh: An Open Framework for Governed Enterprise AI Agents**

Core message:

Enterprise AI is moving beyond passive copilots toward task-specific agents embedded into business workflows. But most organizations still lack the governance, evaluation, data readiness, and architecture needed to scale agents safely.

Open Enterprise AgentOps Mesh is my open-source initiative to help teams design, govern, evaluate, and scale domain-specific enterprise agents.

The first release includes:

- AgentOps reference architecture
- Governance framework
- Evaluation scorecard
- Data-readiness model
- Business use-case catalog
- Guided simulator
- Open-source roadmap

The goal is simple: move from GenAI pilots to measurable, production-grade agentic systems.

## Posting cadence

Recommended cadence:

- 2 posts per week
- 1 long-form article every 2 weeks
- 1 architecture diagram post per week
- 1 simulator/demo update per release

## Authority-building assets

Create:

- One architecture diagram
- One governance matrix
- One agent readiness scorecard
- One data-readiness checklist
- One procurement agent case study
- One executive summary PDF later

## Tone

Use:

- Practical
- Enterprise-aware
- Evidence-based
- Architecture-oriented
- Non-hype
- Clear business-value framing

Avoid:

- Overclaiming autonomy
- Saying agents will replace all employees
- Vendor tribalism
- Model hype
- Weak generic AI slogans

## Industry-presence objective

The goal is not only visibility. The goal is to be associated with a specific professional category:

> Enterprise AgentOps / Agentic AI Governance / AI Transformation Architecture

This category is specific enough to differentiate, but broad enough to sustain future consulting and product opportunities.
